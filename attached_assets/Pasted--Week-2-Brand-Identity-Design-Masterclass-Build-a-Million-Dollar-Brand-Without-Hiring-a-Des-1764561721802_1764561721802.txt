### Create Multiple Professional Logo Options in Hours

**Duration:** 90 minutes

1. **Canva AI** - For logo variations and mockups
1. **Midjourney** - For artistic, unique logos (advanced)
1. **Vectorizer.AI** - Convert to scalable formats

#### Understanding Logo Fundamentals

**The 5 Types of Logos:**

1. **Wordmark** - Text-based (Google, Coca-Cola)
- Best for: Unique business names, service businesses
1. **Lettermark** - Initials (IBM, HBO, CNN)
- Best for: Long business names, professional services
1. **Icon/Symbol** - Graphic only (Apple, Twitter)
- Best for: Established brands, memorable concepts
1. **Combination** - Icon + Text (Adidas, Burger King)
- Best for: Most businesses, maximum flexibility
1. **Emblem** - Text inside symbol (Starbucks, Harley Davidson)
- Best for: Traditional industries, badges/seals

**What Makes a Great Logo:**

- ✅ Simple and memorable
- ✅ Works in black and white
- ✅ Scales from tiny to huge
- ✅ Reflects your brand personality
- ✅ Stands out from competitors
- ✅ Timeless (not trendy)

#### Step-by-Step: Creating Your Logo

**Method 1: Using Looka (Easiest)**

1. **Go to Looka.com**
1. **Enter Your Business Info:**

1. **Review AI Suggestions:**
- Looka generates 100+ options

- Favorite your top 10
1. **Customize Your Favorite:**
- Adjust colors
- Change fonts
- Modify icon placement
- Test different layouts
1. **Download Your Package:**
- Full color version
- Black and white
- Transparent background
- Social media sizes

#### Creating Your Brand Color Palette

- **30% Secondary Color** - Complementary color
- **10% Accent Color** - For CTAs and highlights

- **Neutrals** - White, black, grays for backgrounds and text
- **Variations** - Light and dark versions of your colors

## 📚 Lesson 3: Typography & Font Pairing

### Choose Fonts That Speak Your Brand Language

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### What You’ll Learn:

- Font psychology and personality
- Creating font pairings that work
- Where to find free commercial fonts
- Using fonts consistently

#### Font Categories & Psychology

**Serif Fonts** (with decorative strokes)

- Personality: Traditional, trustworthy, established, elegant
- Use for: Law, finance, luxury, publishing, classic brands
- Examples: Georgia, Playfair Display, Merriweather

**Sans-Serif Fonts** (clean, no strokes)

- Personality: Modern, clean, approachable, minimal
- Use for: Tech, startups, contemporary, casual brands
- Examples: Helvetica, Montserrat, Poppins, Inter

**Script Fonts** (handwritten style)

- Personality: Elegant, feminine, creative, personal
- Use for: Weddings, beauty, boutiques, artistic brands
- Examples: Pacifico, Dancing Script, Great Vibes

**Display Fonts** (decorative, unique)

- Personality: Bold, creative, attention-grabbing, unique
- Use for: Headlines, logos, special occasions
- Examples: Bebas Neue, Righteous, Archivo Black

**Monospace Fonts** (fixed width)

- Personality: Technical, precise, modern, digital
- Use for: Tech, coding, data, contemporary brands
- Examples: Courier, Roboto Mono, Source Code Pro

#### The Font Pairing Formula

**Rule 1: Contrast is Key**
Pair different font styles:

- Serif headline + Sans-serif body
- Script headline + Sans-serif body
- Display headline + Serif or Sans-serif body

**Rule 2: Limit Your Fonts**
Use 2-3 fonts maximum:

- Headline/Display font
- Body/Paragraph font
- Accent font (optional)

**Rule 3: Same Font Family**
Or use one font family with different weights:

- Bold for headlines
- Regular for body
- Light for captions

#### Perfect Font Pairings (Tested & Proven)

**Elegant & Sophisticated:**

- Headlines: Playfair Display (Serif)
- Body: Lato (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Luxury, refined, classic

**Modern & Clean:**

- Headlines: Montserrat Bold (Sans-Serif)
- Body: Open Sans (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Contemporary, professional, approachable

**Creative & Feminine:**

- Headlines: Cormorant Garamond (Serif)
- Body: Raleway (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Artistic, elegant, feminine

**Bold & Confident:**

- Headlines: Bebas Neue (Display)
- Body: Roboto (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Strong, attention-grabbing, modern

**Friendly & Approachable:**

- Headlines: Quicksand (Sans-Serif Rounded)
- Body: Source Sans Pro (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Warm, welcoming, casual

**Tech & Innovation:**

- Headlines: Space Grotesk (Sans-Serif)
- Body: Inter (Sans-Serif)
- Vibe: Cutting-edge, digital, modern

#### Finding Free Fonts

**Google Fonts** (best free source)

- All fonts are free for commercial use
- Easy to use on websites
- High quality and professional

**Font Squirrel** (curated free fonts)

- 100% free for commercial use
- Quality-checked selections
- Desktop and web fonts

**DaFont** (huge selection)

- Check license for commercial use
- Great for display/decorative fonts
- Test before committing

```

Industry: [your field]
Target Audience: [who you serve]
Desired Feeling: [how you want to be perceived]

Color Palette: [your colors from Lesson 2]
Logo Style: [describe your logo]

Suggest:
1. A headline/display font with personality
2. A body/paragraph font for readability
3. An optional accent font
4. Why each font works for my brand
5. How to use them (sizing, weights, where)

```

#### Your Typography System

**Define these:**

**Headline Font:**

- Font name: [e.g., Montserrat]
- Weights to use: [e.g., Bold, Black]
- Use for: Main headlines, hero text, CTAs

**Body Font:**

- Font name: [e.g., Open Sans]
- Weights to use: [e.g., Regular, Semi-Bold]
- Use for: Paragraphs, descriptions, captions

**Font Sizes:**

- H1 (Main headline): 48-72px
- H2 (Subheadline): 32-42px
- H3 (Section title): 24-28px
- Body text: 16-18px
- Small text: 12-14px

**Line Height:**

- Headlines: 1.2-1.3
- Body text: 1.5-1.7

**Letter Spacing:**

- Headlines: -0.02em (tighter)
- Body: 0em (normal)
- All caps: 0.05em (wider)

## 💪 Overcoming Week 2 Challenges

**“I’m not creative enough for this!”**

- You ARE creative - you just proved it in Week 1

- Create a mood board of brands you admire
- Ask your ideal clients what resonates
- Trust your gut - you know your brand
- Your brand will evolve as you do
